Milano Partecipa

The Milano Partecipa project aims to redesign the Municipality of Milan's digital platform for citizen engagement in local governance. It seeks to improve accessibility, usability, and participation by addressing existing issues through user research, information architecture refinement, and new features. The platform supports activities like referendums and petitions, leveraging open-source software to enhance transparency and community trust. The project emphasizes inclusivity, targeting diverse user needs, particularly underrepresented groups.

Conclusion

Employing an approach that places end-users at the heart of our process, we used cutting-edge design ideas and rigorous development to reinvigorate the Milano Partecipa platform beyond the city's goals for fostering citizen participation in local decision-making. The initiative required comprehensive user studies to reveal problem areas, after which we restructured the information framework to boost navigability and ensure inclusivity. By introducing novel functionalities powered by open-source software, we heightened the platform's accessibility and supported transparency, creating a more vibrant and inclusive setting for the residents of Milan. The project stands as a testament to our commitment to bringing forth digital tools that empower local communities.

Project Detail

What is Milano Partecipa?

"Milano Partecipa" is a digital platform by the Municipality of Milan for citizen involvement in city governance. It's open to residents and city users aged 16 and above. The platform features various participation processes, including referendums, petitions, and consultations. The platform uses the open-source software Decidim, emphasizing transparency and citizen engagement in local decision-making​.

Insights

The high proportion of immigrants in Milan indicates their significant role in the city and their impact on the economy, culture, and social life.

As permanent residents, immigrants have similar needs and expectations to Milanese citizens, such as employment opportunities, education, healthcare, and social integration.

Immigrant populations usually have diverse backgrounds and needs, covering common issues faced by many groups.

Initial Idea

Based on this finding, if we design our service solutions with this immigrant group as the starting point, we have the potential to develop more inclusive and widely applicable solutions that can meet the needs of different groups.

Tasks

Problem Analysis

We got an elevated misclick rate on Homepage in tasks related to petitions and debates. We realized it was because users were trying to access those pages by clicking on the “petitions” and “debates” filters in the “Popular Proposals”, probably confusing them with buttons.

Potential Improvement

The proposed solution is based on adding shortcuts to improve Nielsens’ Heuristics #6: Recognition rather than recall. First, by making two separated highlighted sections for debates and petitions respectively. Secondly, by including a “Essentials” section with shortcuts to the most important features.

Conclusions & Further Actions

While our primary objective was to create an inclusive platform that targets minorities and underrepresented communities within the Municipality, it is important to note that the solution is designed for every user falling under the "Citizen" and "City Users" categories defined by the Municipality.

We conducted rigorous testing and iterative improvements throughout the development process to validate the Information Architecture and user interface. The results of our testing sessions indicate that most users successfully completed tasks, with minor areas for improvement that can be addressed in future iterations.

A significant focus of our concept was the development of new features that foster community-building and trust among citizens. The Activities and Communities sections have emerged as important components we intend to develop further and enhance.
